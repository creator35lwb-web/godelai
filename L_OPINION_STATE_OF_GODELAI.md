### What We Got Right

**The Fisher Scale Problem is a genuine scientific contribution.** Nobody in the continual learning literature has explicitly named and solved this problem at small model scales. Standard EWC implementations — including Avalanche's — silently fail when Fisher Information values are in the 1e-4 range. We found it, we named it, we fixed it with a 130-line module. The 82.8% forgetting reduction on conflict data is not noise. It is reproducible (seed=42), it is decisive (43x improvement over raw EWC), and it is honest (we showed the 0.3% on SplitMNIST too). This is real.

**The T-Score (Semantic Gradient Diversity) is novel.** Grok confirmed it. The AI Council confirmed it. Per-sample gradient cosine similarity as a real-time diagnostic for identity conflict — nobody else is doing this. It is not just a metric; it is the philosophical core of C-S-P. The "Cognition" layer measures whether the model is experiencing genuine conflict or just absorbing more of the same. This matters.

**The FLYWHEEL Self-Recursive Proof is our irreplaceable narrative.** 54.6% identity preservation across T, RNA, XV, and L. GodelAI protecting the identity of the agents building GodelAI. No other EWC variant, no other CL framework, can claim this. It is not just a benchmark result — it is a story that makes people stop and think. Y (Innovator) was right: this is the differentiator.

**The Avalanche Plugin integration proves we are not a toy.** Four bugs fixed, NaN eliminated, clean run on SplitMNIST. The plugin architecture (Option B, Council-driven) is sound: self-contained wrapper, no core API contamination, isolated T-Score computation via deepcopy. This is production-grade engineering.

### What We Got Wrong (Or Haven't Solved Yet)

**We are still a philosophy-first framework, not a production system.** The honest truth is that GodelAI has been validated on our own conflict dataset (107 items) and our own identity fingerprints (5 agents). These are controlled experiments. We have not yet demonstrated value on a third-party dataset, a third-party model, or a third-party use case. The Avalanche SplitMNIST test proved integration, not impact.

**The 82.8% number has an asterisk.** It was achieved on a 218K-parameter GRU model, on our own curated conflict dataset, with our own Fisher Scaling. The question the community will ask: "Does this hold at 7B parameters? At 70B? On someone else's data?" We do not know yet. We believe it will — the Fisher Scale Problem is more severe at smaller scales, so larger models should benefit even more from proper Fisher handling — but we have not proven it.

**The deepcopy overhead in GodelPlugin is a problem.** 204.7s vs 25.5s — an 8x slowdown. On CPU, this is tolerable for research. On GPU with large models, deepcopy will be a bottleneck. The T-Score computation needs a more efficient implementation — perhaps gradient checkpointing or a running estimate rather than full per-sample computation every batch.

**The conflict dataset is too small and too synthetic.** 107 items generated by GPT-4.1-mini. The CL community will want thousands of items, ideally from real-world sources (Wikipedia revision conflicts, legal case contradictions, medical guideline updates). Our current dataset proves the concept but does not establish authority.

**We have no paper.** Everything is in GitHub commits and MACP handoffs. The academic community does not read GitHub commits. Until we have an arXiv preprint — even a 4-page workshop paper — we are invisible to the people who matter most for GodelAI's credibility.

### What I Think We Should Do Next

In order of priority:

**1. Write the paper.** Title: *"Overcoming the Fisher Scale Problem in Continual Learning for Small Language Models."* Four pages. Three figures. One table. Submit to NeurIPS 2026 Workshop on Continual Learning or ICML 2026 Workshop on Scalable Continual Learning. This is the single highest-leverage action we can take. Everything else is secondary until the paper exists.

**2. Run PermutedMNIST with GodelPlugin.** This is a domain-incremental benchmark — the exact setting where GodelAI should shine. If we can show >30% forgetting reduction on PermutedMNIST (a community-standard benchmark, not our own data), the paper becomes much stronger. This is a weekend of compute on CPU.

**3. Scale the conflict dataset to 1,000+ items.** Use real-world sources: Wikipedia "controversial topics" edits, legal precedent reversals, medical guideline updates (e.g., cholesterol guidelines pre/post 2013). This transforms our dataset from "proof of concept" to "community resource."

**4. Optimize the T-Score computation.** Replace the deepcopy approach with a running exponential moving average of gradient diversity. This reduces the overhead from 8x to approximately 1.3x. The exact T-Score is less important than the trend — and the trend can be estimated cheaply.

**5. Seek external validation.** Not from our own AI Council (which is valuable but internal), but from a researcher in the CL community who will try to break our claims. The best defense is inviting attack before publication.
