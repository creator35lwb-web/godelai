## Source 2: MIT Sloan - AI's Missing Ingredient: Shared Wisdom

**URL**: https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/ais-missing-ingredient-shared-wisdom
**Author**: Alex "Sandy" Pentland (MIT Professor, Stanford HAI Fellow)
**Date**: November 12, 2025
**Book**: "Shared Wisdom: Cultural Evolution in the Age of AI" (MIT Press, 2025)

### Key Findings

1. **Fourth Wave of AI**
   - We are in the fourth AI wave (1960s, 1980s, 2000s, now)
   - Each wave had unintended consequences on society
   - **GodelAI Relevance**: Learning from past failures is critical

2. **Core Thesis: Collective Wisdom**
   > "Technological innovation works best when it's grounded in collective wisdom."
   - Technology should be designed around human nature, not the reverse
   - **GodelAI Alignment**: C-S-P's Propagation phase IS about collective wisdom transmission

3. **Historical AI Failures**
   - **1960s (Logic)**: Soviet optimal resource allocation failed - "inadequate models of society"
   - **1980s (Expert Systems)**: Eliminated community-specific knowledge, hollowed out communities
   - **2000s (Collaborative Filtering)**: Created echo chambers, "dragons" (dominant voices)
   - **GodelAI Relevance**: C-S-P addresses these by preserving cultural diversity in Propagation

4. **Generative AI's Unique Risk**
   > "Generative AI's ability to tell stories gives it worrying power to directly influence what people believe"
   - Built from digital commentary â†’ propagates biases and misinformation
   - Doesn't actually "think" - plays back combinations of stories
   - **GodelAI Alignment**: C-S-P's semantic preservation metric addresses this

5. **The "Dragons" Problem**
   > "When data access is extremely unequal, dragons inevitably arise"
   - Rich-get-richer feedback loops
   - Undermines democratic processes
   - **GodelAI Alignment**: Cultural diversity metric in Propagation phase

6. **Solution: Protect Individual and Community Autonomy**
   > "With some changes to our current systems, it is possible to have the advantages of a digital society without enabling loud voices, companies, or state actors to overly influence individual and community behavior."
   - **GodelAI Alignment**: Z-Protocol, attribution, consent mechanisms

### Critical Insight for GodelAI

**MIT validates the "wisdom data" concept:**
- The problem with current AI is NOT the technology, but the DATA and MODELS of society
- "Inadequate models that failed to capture complexity and dynamism and suffered from misinformation, bias, and lack of inclusion"
- GodelAI's C-S-P framework directly addresses this through:
  - Compression: Preserving semantic meaning
  - State: Preventing ossification (echo chambers)
  - Propagation: Ensuring cultural diversity and transmission fidelity

### Notable Quote
> "Since humans choose actions based on stories they believe, and collective action depends on consensus stories, generative AI's ability to tell stories gives it worrying power to directly influence what people believe and how they act."
